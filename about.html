About Fathom

Eleven PDF and Word tools, built so you don't have to hand your files to a stranger's server just to merge two PDFs.

What this is

Fathom is a small, independent set of PDF and Word tools — merge, split, compress, organize, convert, watermark, number, and now convert to and from Word — built for the kind of file-shuffling that comes up constantly during school: combining lecture notes, shrinking a scan before it can be submitted, pulling three slides out of a deck, or turning a PDF into something you can actually edit.

Why it works the way it does

Most free PDF sites work by uploading your file to a server, processing it there, and sending it back. That's normal, but it means your file — which might be a transcript, an essay draft, or a scanned ID — passes through someone else's computer along the way. Fathom does the opposite: every tool runs as JavaScript in your own browser, using open-source libraries (pdf-lib, pdf.js, JSZip, and docx) to read and rewrite your file locally. Nothing is uploaded, because there's nowhere for it to go — there's no server behind this site at all.

Who it's for

Built with students in mind, but there's nothing student-specific about it — anyone who needs quick, no-nonsense PDF or Word editing without creating an account or wondering where their file just went is welcome to use it.
